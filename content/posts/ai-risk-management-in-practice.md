 Picture this. You're working with a 400-person consulting firm that operates across healthcare, financial services, and government. AI is everywhere. Custom models trained on proprietary client data. Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems (AI that searches a company's internal documents and uses that information to generate answers) embedded in internal knowledge tools. AI-assisted hiring. Automated contract analysis. Predictive analytics delivered straight to C-suite clients.

Now someone asks you to figure out where the risks are. Not in theory, but in practice. Which systems could cause the most damage if something goes wrong? What framework do you apply? And how do you build a governance plan that actually fits a firm this size without overengineering it?

That's the kind of problem that lands on your desk in an AI governance role. And it's exactly what I worked through in a recent risk assessment.
