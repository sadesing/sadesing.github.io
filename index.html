About Me

I'm a proud alumna of The State University of New York at Buffalo and Texas Southern University, and my work focuses on the intersection of technology and human needs. I enjoy developing solutions, whether it's crafting user-friendly websites or improving digital accessibility, that are both aesthetically pleasing and inclusive for everyone.

My background in human factors and ergonomics sparked my passion for helping individuals with disabilities. Analyzing how people interact with systems, products, and physical environments to optimize usability and performance gave me a strong foundation in user-centered design — which I now apply to the digital realm.

Beyond my professional work, I'm committed to empowering underrepresented communities in STEM. I serve as the Community Outreach Lead for Black Girls CODE Dallas and the Webmaster for Engineers Without Borders North Texas. I previously connected with amazing communities as an Evangelist for Women Who Code DFW. In my free time, I enjoy traveling, sharing moments with loved ones, exploring different cuisines, and pursuing my interest in photography.

I strive to be inspired, to envision what others might deem impossible, to dedicate myself to meaningful work, and to cultivate relationships with those who inspire me to be my best self.
